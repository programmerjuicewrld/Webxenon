Consultancy

We consult and advise work to ensure clients' businesses or enterprises are running as efficiently as possible. We are aware of the business and legislative implications of our advice. We usually consult on either technical or business matters.

Types of Agricultural consultancy we provide

Technical consultants provide specialist advice on:

  • Agronomic practices
  • Irrigation management
  • Soil management
  • Pest and disease management
  • Environment and conservation
  • Diary production and management
  • Poultry production and management
  • Animal nutrition and management
  • Agro processing
  • Gender
  • Nutrition
  • Waste management
  • Enviroment management plan
  • Farm audit and certification
  • Other technical applications.
Business consultants help with:
  • Business planning and feasibility study
  • Estate and financial management advice for agricultural businesses and farms
  • Business analytics using data science platforms
  • Personnel managment

Our responsibilitiese

include:

  • Visiting clients to identify and evaluate their business and/or technical requirements
  • Assist clients with business planning, planning applications, government grant applications, legislative advice and new business ventures
  • Collect and analyze data, crop yield and financial reports to measure performance
  • Prepare or modify business or operating plans
  • Organize and conduct field trials to find solutions to clients' problems
  • Plan and implement improvements for the client such as using more effective pest control measures or finding more efficient ways to keep livestock
  • Organize presentations, demonstrations, training and farm walks for clients, colleagues, partner organizations, professional bodies and other interested groups
  • Communicate effectively, both in writing and orally, with clients, colleagues and members of the public
  • Write advisory leaflets, technical notes and possibly press releases and articles
  • Market and promote consultancy services to new customers, while maintaining existing client relationships
  • Research and keep up to date with any relevant developments in agriculture
  • Carry out administrative duties, manage budgets and accounts, update information and prepare reports.

Our Clients are:

  • Farmers
  • Growers
  • NGO's
  • Public offices
  • Other agricultural businesses and services.
